üìà Progressive Overload Rules

The Golden Rule: When you can comfortably complete all sets and reps for an exercise at the target Hardness of REP, increase the weight by the smallest available increment in the next session.

Progression Guidelines:

  • Month 1: Focus on form mastery, small increments (1-2,5 kg upper body, 2,5-5 kg lower body)
  • Month 2: Prioritize progressive overload, larger increments (2,5-5 kg upper body, 5-7 kg lower body)
  • Month 3: Push intensity while maintaining form, focus on intensity techniques

LISS => Low-Intensity: The exercise feels easy and comfortable, and you can sustain it for a long time.

HIIT => High-Intensity: The exercise feels challenging and uncomfortable, and you can't sustain it for a long time.

HR => Heart Rate: You can estimate your maximum heart rate by subtracting your age from 220 .

üß† Mindset & Motivation

Your physical transformation is a mental game, too. Stay consistent and be patient with the process. A single workout won't make you strong, but a consistent habit of training will.

Tips for Success:

  • Celebrate Small Wins: Be proud of every workout you complete and every extra rep you get.
  • Listen to Your Body: Some days you'll feel great, others you won't. It's okay to take a rest day or deload when needed.
  • Focus on Form: Perfecting your technique is more important than lifting a heavy weight with poor form.

ü§∏ Warm-up & Cool-down

Don't skip these! A proper warm-up prepares your body for the workout and prevents injury. A cool-down aids in recovery.

Warm-up (5-10 minutes):

  • Light Cardio: 5 minutes on a treadmill, elliptical, or bike to get your heart rate up.
  • Dynamic Stretches: Arm circles, leg swings, torso twists.
  • Specific Warm-up: Do 1-2 sets of the first exercise with very light weight to practice the movement.

Cool-down (5-10 minutes):

  • Static Stretches: Hold stretches for your quads, hamstrings, glutes, and chest for 20-30 seconds each.

üìö Glossary

RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion)

A scale from 1-10 to rate the intensity of your set. RPE 7 means you feel like you could have done 3 more reps. RPE 9 means you could have done 1 more rep.

Progressive Overload

The principle of gradually increasing the stress on your body during training. This can be done by increasing weight, reps, sets, or decreasing rest time.

Mesocycle

A training period, usually 4-6 weeks, with a specific focus (e.g., strength, endurance). This program is divided into three mesocycles.
